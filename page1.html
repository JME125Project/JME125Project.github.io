The Trolley Problem

Steering the Trolley into the One Individual

Overview

The trolley problem is a well-known ethical dilemma. In the scenario where one must steer a trolley to avoid killing five individuals but in doing so causes the death of one individual, proponents of steering into the one often cite utilitarian ethics. This perspective prioritizes minimizing harm by saving the greatest number of lives.

Utilitarianism, a consequentialist framework, argues that actions should be judged based on their outcomes. By this reasoning, actively steering the trolley to kill one person rather than passively allowing five to die is morally justifiable because it results in the least overall harm. This decision emphasizes the moral weight of numbers while grappling with the difficulty of taking direct action to harm an individual.
