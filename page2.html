The Trolley Problem

Choosing Not to Act

Overview

In the trolley problem, some argue that it is morally better to refrain from steering the trolley, even though this means five individuals will die. This perspective often relies on deontological ethics which emphasizes the importance of moral rules over consequences. From this viewpoint, intentionally diverting the trolley to kill one person constitutes an unethical act of intentional harm, violating the principle that individuals should not be treated as a means to an end.

By not taking action, the individual avoids direct responsibility for causing harm, as the trolley's current path and consequences were predetermined. This view prioritizes the moral rule that one should not deliberately harm others, even if inaction leads to greater harm. Supporters of this stance argue that the morality of an action depends on the action itself, not its outcomes.
